A LEGO Brick Sorter

Every night 3-5 employees sit on the floor of the LEGOLand Discovery Center and hand sort the dirty brick by color. It is an inefficent, unsanitary and degrating process that needs to be automated.

This is why we built the automatic LEGO Brick Sorter! Our machine is an integrated mechanical and electrical system that also uses computer vision to sort the bricks by color and shape. It is also fun to watch and could make the perfect new addition to any LEGOLand Discovery Center!

Automated

Sorting bricks by color is a demeaning task that even a robot could do, so we built one!

Sanitary

Our machine reduces exposure to the germ-harboring bricks, thus keeping the employees healthier.

Economical

LEGOLand employees spend over every night sorting bricks. With our machine they could save over a thousand dollars a month!

The System

Mixed bricks are poured into the isolator the top. The when the box is spun via the hand-crank on the side, the LEGOs tumble around inside, and fall through the hole and into the scoop. The scoop drops them onto the slides so the bricks can move on to the next phase.

The slide transports the bricks from the tilter to the cups.

Slide

The scanner uses OpenCV to detect the shape and color of the brick. A mount holds a camera above the brick and the software then processes that image and classifies the type of brick present. Then it tells the Tilter and the Spinner to move to the correct positon.

Scanner

The tilter transfers bricks from the scanner onto the slide. First, the plate holds a brick while it is being scanned, and then tips to drop them one direction or the other, based on the outcome of the scan.

Tilter

At the very end of the process, the LEGOs are sorted into cups spread out on a disc that rotates to put the correct cups in position to receive each brick.

Spinner

The frame holds up the pegboard at the correct angle.
